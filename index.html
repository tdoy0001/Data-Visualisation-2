Australian Wine


Overview

Australia is one of the leading wine-producing countries in the world, known for its high-quality wines and diverse wine regions. Its wine industry is a significant part of the country's agricultural and export sectors, with wines being sold domestically and internationally.

Wine Regions

Australia boasts over 60 designated wine regions, each with its unique climate and soil, contributing to the diversity of wines produced.

South Australia and New South Wales are known for their warmer climate varieties such as Shiraz and Cabernet Sauvignon, whereas Victoria is known for cool climate-loving Pinot Noir.

Major wine regions include Barossa Valley in South Australia, Hunter Valley in New South Wales, and Margaret River in Western Australia.

Wine Varieties

In Australia, the move toward French wine varieties has been dramatic. This is because varieties known for producing premium still and sparkling wines are now preferred over those traditionally used for fortified wines.

Producers are well aware of the impact climate changes are having on the quality their winegrapes and on vineyard yields and production costs.

Adaptation strategies include switching to warmer-climate or more-resilient grape varieties, and re-locating to a region at a higher latitude or elevation to retain the current mix of grape varieties in their portfolio.

Consumption

Apparent consumption of alcohol refers to the total amount of alcohol produced or imported that will be sold to people living in Australia.

Per capita wine consumption remains at one of the highest levels in the last 60 years, and accounted for 42% of the total apparent alcohol consumption, making it the largest category.

Beer consumption continued its long-standing decline, dropping to 3.5 litres of pure alcohol consumed per person in 2020, marking the lowest level since the 1940s.

Spirits saw a 19% increase in availability from 1.8 litres of alcohol per capita in 2017 to 2.1 litres in 2020.

Cider continues to make up a small portion of the total amount of alcohol made available in Australia.

Source: Australian Institute of Health and Welfare



Imports and Exports

Australian wine has gained global recognition,and are now exported to over 100 countries, making it one of the top wine-exporting nations.

In 2023, 621 million litres of wine were exported, valued at $1.86 billion. The top destination market by value was the UK with 19%, closely followed by the US, also with 19%.

In the domestic market, Australian wine sales makes up 82% of sales, with an estimated 444 million litres.
